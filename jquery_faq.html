Joshua National Park

  • Designated national monument in 1936 by Franklin Delano Roosevelt.
  • Accorded national park status in 1994.
  • Named for the Joshua Trees (Yucca brevifolia) native to the park.
  • Covers an area of 3,200 square kilometers

Glacier National Park

  • World's first international peace park.
  • Evidence of human use in the area dates back over 12,000 years.
  • Has welcomed over 100 million visitors to the park.
  • Largest glacier in the park is 0.7 square miles.

Grand Teton National Park

  • 40-mile-long Teton Range is the youngest range in the Rocky Mountains.
  • There's a commercial airport on park grounds.
  • Pronghorns reside there, which are the fastest land animal in the western hemisphere.
  • Park once provoked a cattle-drive protest.
